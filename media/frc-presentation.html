Functional organisation of networks

Complex networks: structure

  • Networks represent complex systems of many interacting units

We can focus

  • on the structure (topology) of the pairwise interactions → patterns in the relation \(E\) on \(V\)

    \(G = (V, E)\) with \(E \in V \times V\) (given \(i, j \in V\), \(i \sim j\) iff \((i, j) \in E\))

  • but also on what happens in these interactions

What do interactions represent?

  • E.g. e-mail exchange, physical contact, people moving from one place to another, electrical or neuro-physiological or chemical signal…
  • Exchange of information, communication

Why? Communicating… for which reason?

Interactions as communication, exchange of information, signal

Complex networks: function

  • Systems have/carry on a phenotypic function (or multiple ones)
    • e.g. navigation/transport of people and goods, economy, cognition, life…
  • Assumption: pairwise communication as the basic factor for the emergence of the system’s phenotypic function
  • Studying the evolution of the communication (dynamics on networks) to understand how the pairwise interactions shape higher-order interactions (e.g. between groups) and, ultimately, understand the system’s behaviour and function

Note the ambiguity: function…

The rich-club phenomenon

An excess connectivity among nodes with high degree (the rich nodes or hubs) that cannot be explained by pure chance [7,8]

The (structural) rich-club

  • Rich-club coefficient [8], density of connections among nodes with degree larger than \(k\):

\[\phi(k) = \frac{2E_{>k}}{N_{>k} (N_{>k} - 1)}\]

where \(N_{>k}\) is the number of nodes with degree larger than \(k\) and \(E_{>k}\) is the number of edges between them.

  • Normalisation needed!

The (structural) rich-club

  • Rich-club coefficient: \(\phi(k) = \frac{2E_{>k}}{N_{>k} (N_{>k} - 1)}\)
  • Rich-club ratio [9], normalization to account for degree-degree correlations.

\[\rho_{\text{ran}}(k) = \frac{\phi(k)}{\phi_{\text{ran}}(k)}\]

Some definitions - 1

Given a network \(G=(V, E)\), the diffusion distance [3] between two nodes \(i, j \in V\) is defined as

\[ D_t(i, j) = || \mathbf{p}(t | i) - \mathbf{p}(t | j) ||_2 \]

where \(\mathbf{p}(t | i)\) is the (posterior) probability of finding the random walker in any node (node \(k\) ↔︎ \(k-\)th component) at time \(t>0\), given that:

  1. it started at node \(i\) at time \(t=0\) with probability 1 and
  2. it follows a continuous-time classical random walk on the network \(G\).

FRC (intuition)

  • If rich nodes are nearer to each other than expected by chance, accounting also for the rest of the nodes in the network, then there exists a functional rich-club.

  • Small diffusion distances \(D_t\) between rich-nodes mean that it is easy for two random walkers starting in different rich nodes to meet somewhere in the network at time \(t\)

  • If these distances are smaller than expected in comparison with the configuration model, then the (diffusion) proximity of rich nodes is not due to the large degrees of the nodes.

Some definitions - 2

To define the functional rich-club coefficient (FRCC)

  • Remove effect of the network: \(\tilde{D}_t(i, j) = \frac{D_t(i, j) - \mu({D_t})}{\sigma({D_t})}\) \({D}_t(i, j) \stackrel{not.}{=} \tilde{D}_t(i, j)\)
  • Consider distances among the \(k-\)richest nodes \(\left\{{D}_t(i, j) : i, j\in G, k_i, k_j \geq k\right\}\) and compute their average, \(\mu\left({D}_t \big|_{\geq k}\right)\), called the average core (diffusion) distance of \(G\) at level \(k\)
  • Do the same for a sample from the configuration model [10], computing also the standard deviation of these distances: \(m\left(D_t^{\text{cm}} \big|_{\geq k}\right)\), \(s\left(D_t^{\text{cm}} \big|_{\geq k}\right)\)

  • Define the functional rich-club coefficient

    \[\text{FRCC}_{t, k} = \frac{m\left(D_t^{\text{cm}} \big|_{\geq k}\right) - \mu\left({D}_t \big|_{\geq k}\right)}{s\left(D_t^{\text{cm}} \big|_{\geq k}\right)}\]

Conclusions & future work

  • Not only the absence of a structural rich-club, but also the presence of clear meso-scale structures, does not facilitate or even hinders the diffusion between rich nodes
  • This is just a first attempt toward a functional characterisation of this (and other) sub-networks
  • Future: account for other communication types
